The Safety Hierarchy

The Safety Hierarchy, also known as the Hierarchy of Controls, is a systematic approach in occupational health and safety that prioritizes hazard control measures from the most effective to the least effective in order to reduce the risk of accidents and occupational diseases. It emphasizes that hazards should be addressed at their source whenever possible, rather than relying solely on individual behavior or personal protection.

Elimination is the most effective level of control, where the hazard is completely removed from the workplace so that the risk no longer exists.

Substitution involves replacing a hazardous process, material, or activity with one that is less dangerous, thereby reducing the overall risk.

Engineering controls focus on isolating people from hazards through physical or technical means by modifying equipment, processes, or the work environment.

‚Ä¢ Administrative Controls

Administrative controls aim to change the way people work by implementing policies, procedures, training, supervision, and work practices that reduce exposure to hazards.

PPE is the last line of defense and provides protection directly to the worker; it does not eliminate the hazard but minimizes the potential impact of exposure.
