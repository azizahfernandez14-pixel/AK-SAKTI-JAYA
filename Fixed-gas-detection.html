Fixed Gas Detection Regulations

Fixed Gas Detection Systems play a critical role in monitoring hazardous gas concentrations in industrial and high-risk environments. In Indonesia, their implementation is governed by Law No. 1 of 1970 on Occupational Safety, which requires employers to control dangerous atmospheres and prevent accidents caused by toxic or flammable gases.

These requirements are reinforced by Government Regulation No. 50 of 2012 (SMK3), mandating hazard identification, risk assessment, and the use of engineering controls such as fixed gas detection and alarm systems. Additionally, Ministry of Manpower Regulation No. 5 of 2018 establishes exposure limits and the need for continuous monitoring in workplaces with potential gas hazards. Compliance with applicable SNI and international standards supports reliable system performance and effective risk prevention.

Lifeline System
