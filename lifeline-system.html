Working at Height Regulations

Working at height is a high-risk activity that must comply with Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) regulations in Indonesia. Under Law No. 1 of 1970 and Ministry of Manpower Regulation No. 9 of 2016, companies are required to implement effective fall risk control through proper work planning, the use of fall protection systems, and competent personnel. These requirements are further reinforced by Ministry of Manpower Regulation No. 8 of 2010, which mandates the provision of personal protective equipment in accordance with applicable safety standards. Lifeline System

The Lifeline System is a safety device designed to protect workers from the risk of falling while working at heights. This system acts as a secure restraint or anchor, keeping workers protected even if they lose their balance or slip.
